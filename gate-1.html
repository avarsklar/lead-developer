Gate 1 — Stop being scared to touch it

Are you here yet? If the thought of changing your live app makes you nervous — what if I break it, what if I lose my work — then yes, you're here. So is everyone else: every app any senior engineer has ever built started exactly here. It's not a grade or a failure.

The fear this kills: "If I touch it, I'll break it, or lose my work."

Two skills. That's the whole gate.

Tap a skill's name to open its own page; each has a short 20-second video.

/safety-net — run once

A floor you can't fall through: a saved working version you can return to in one move, your secret keys moved out of the code (AI often pastes them right in) and into a password manager, and automatic backups you've watched actually restore. It takes 20–30 minutes for a simple app, closer to an hour if you have a real database. The full list is on its page.

/ship-change — run every single time you change anything

The everyday loop, for any change — a button color, a typo, a whole feature. Try it on a private copy first, look at it, and publish only when you say okay. A fresh way back comes with every change. Its first run also builds that private copy, so finishing your first change finishes the setup too.

Reality check "Proven" means you watched it happen: the restore actually restoring, the change checked with your own eyes before it went live. Not a setting switched on. That's what makes the calm real instead of borrowed.

That's Gate 1 — for most people, the whole fear, handled. You only need to go further when real money, other people's data, or people counting on the app being up enter the picture.
