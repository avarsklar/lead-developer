# Gate 1 — Stop being scared to touch it

<div class="gatecheck g1" markdown="1">
<span class="q">Are you here yet?</span>
If the thought of changing your live app makes you nervous — *what if I break it, what if I lose my work* — then **yes, you're here.** And so is everyone else: every app every senior engineer has ever built started exactly here. It's not a grade or a failure. It's the trailhead.
</div>

**The fear this kills:** *"If I touch it, I'll break it, or lose my work."*

Two tools. That's the whole gate.

<p class="taphint">Tap a skill's name to open its own page — each has a short 20-second video.</p>

## [`/safety-net`](skill-safety-net.html) — run once

A floor you can't fall through. When it's done you have four things, each **proven**, not just switched on:

- a saved working version you can return to in **one move**,
- your secret passwords pulled out of the code and replaced with brand-new ones, so the exposed old ones are dead and useless,
- automatic backups, and
- a backup you **watched** get restored, with your own eyes — because a backup you've never restored from isn't really a backup.

After this, you literally cannot lose your work and you cannot lose your data.

## [`/ship-change`](skill-ship-change.html) — run *every single time* you change anything

The everyday loop, however small the change — a button color, a typo, a whole feature, doesn't matter: try it on a private copy first, look at it, publish only on your okay — with a fresh way back every time. Its first run quietly builds the private copy `/safety-net` left for it, so by the end of your first change the whole gate is set up *and* you've used it once.

<div class="cal reality" markdown="1">
<span class="lbl">Reality check</span>
"Proven" means **you watched it catch something** — not that a setting is switched on. You don't confirm a net *exists*; you watch it *work*. That's what makes the calm real instead of borrowed.
</div>

**That's Gate 1 — for most people, the whole emergency, handled.** You only need to go further if real money, other people's data, or people *depending on you being up* enters the picture.
