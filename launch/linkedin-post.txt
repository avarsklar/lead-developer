I built a marketplace for my college campus as a passion project. Once real people started using it, it stopped being fun and started scaring me.

I kept hearing about Claude Code and wanted to try it, and selling my own clothes had always been a pain: you lose money to fees, everyone has different taste, and the big resale sites have so many options you can never find what you actually like. So at the end of the day, I'd play around with building something better. I had fun. I was never going to do anything with it. Then my friends found out and pushed me to publish, because they had clothes to sell too.

It's called Bucknell Refit, a marketplace where students buy and sell to each other. And once real people and real money were on it, I realized I had no idea what I was doing.

I've only taken a couple of CS classes. I barely knew what Stripe was. How was I supposed to know payments actually went through and people got their money? I didn't want to get anyone in trouble, or get in trouble with my school. I was scared to even edit the site in case it crashed while someone was on it. I couldn't fix a broken button. I didn't know how to organize any of it.

For the three weeks it was live, it took over my whole world. I couldn't focus on school. I was constantly calling my dad for help. I was afraid of losing people's money, of losing their trust.

Somewhere in all that, I got obsessed: with Claude Code, with building things, with startups. And I started working out how to make the AI I was building with act like the careful senior developer I didn't have, so I could stop being scared to touch my own app.

I turned everything I figured out into a free set of skills, built with two Rice University professors. It's for people who love making things with Claude Code but don't have the skills a senior developer does to protect their app and the people using it. I made it so they can get rid of the fears I had.

Free handbook: https://avarsklar.github.io/lead-developer/
The skills, free on GitHub: https://github.com/avarsklar/lead-developer-skills
