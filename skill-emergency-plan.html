Gate 2 · the 2 a.m. plan

/emergency-plan

Your calm, written-down plan for the night something breaks — so you're not improvising at 2 a.m.

The fear it kills: "One night it'll break, I'll be alone, I won't know what to do — and the only tool I have is the same AI that might make it worse."

What it does

It runs in two parts.

  • Part 1 (15–20 minutes, touches nothing live): a one-page card on every device — is it just me or everyone? how do I get back to working? what do I tell my users? who do I call?
  • Part 1 also writes hard, standing rules for your 2 a.m. AI, so it stays a careful reader on your already-tested way back instead of an improviser.
  • Part 2 (45–90 minutes; recommended now, bookable for later): rehearses the way back for real, builds an in-app banner to tell users, and wires the "your app is down" alarm to your phone.

When I did the practice run on my own app, the undo I was counting on turned out to be broken. It had a bug I never would have found until a real emergency, probably at the worst possible time. We fixed it during the drill and ran it again until I watched it actually work.

When to run it

When you're about to depend on your app being up, or you're scared of the night it breaks. It also fires live if you type something like "it's down."

When to stop or skip

Skip if nobody really depends on it being up yet. Re-check the card and alarm every few months. They rot too.

What you need first

/safety-net: a tested way back is required. Part 2's drill and banner use /ship-change's private copy.

What's next

That's Gate 2 — you're safe for real strangers. If you're growing this into a real, ongoing thing, climb to Gate 3: /release-foundation.
