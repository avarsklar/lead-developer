/release-foundation

Put your new features on a calendar instead of shipping each one the second it's done. Run once.

The fear it kills: "I ship everything the second it's done, and I'm announcing features the night before."

What it does

  • You pick how often you release — every week / 2 weeks / month.
  • It builds — and shows you — a calendar of which feature goes out on which date.
  • A finished feature waits in a holding pen until its day, instead of going live the moment it's built.
  • It teaches the rule that ends the panic: miss the train, catch the next one.

When to run it

Once, when you're growing your app into a real, ongoing thing and putting new features out to real users.

When to stop or skip

Stop if your app isn't live to real strangers yet — a calendar solves a problem you only have once real people depend on you; until then, just keep shipping freely with /ship-change. Also skip if it's a finished personal thing you only tinker with.

Before this, you need

/safety-net and /ship-change.

After this — do this skill

/release — what you run on each release date to put a parked feature live, seatbelts on.
