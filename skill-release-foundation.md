**The fear it kills:** *"I ship everything the second it's done, and I'm announcing features the night before."*

## What it does

- You pick how often you release: every week, every 2 weeks, or monthly.
- It builds a calendar of which feature goes out on which date, and shows it to you before anything else happens.
- A finished feature stays parked until its day.
- A release-day reminder lands on your calendar (Google / Apple / Outlook) at the day and time you pick, so release day never sneaks up on you.
- And it teaches the rule that ends the panic: a feature that misses its date just catches the next release.

## When to run it

Once, when you're growing your app into a real, ongoing thing and putting new features out to real users. Plan a real working session; it's short if you already know what you want to build next.

## When to stop or skip

Stop if your app isn't live to real strangers yet — a calendar solves a problem you only have once real people depend on you. Until then, keep shipping freely with [`/ship-change`](skill-ship-change.html). Also skip if it's a finished personal thing you only tinker with.
